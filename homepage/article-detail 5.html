Overview

Choosing between a current-year and a one-year-old sedan is one of the most common — and most financially consequential — decisions a car buyer faces. The 2025 models bring meaningful updates in safety tech, infotainment, and powertrain efficiency. But 2024 models now carry 8–14% depreciation discounts, making them exceptional value for buyers who don't need the latest features. This guide walks through every dimension that matters so you can make the right call for your situation.

2025 New Model Year

2025 Sedans

Updated safety suites, new infotainment hardware, mild powertrain efficiency gains, and fresh exterior styling on key models. Pay a premium for the latest — but it's more justified than past years.

2024 Best Value

2024 Sedans

Proven reliability data, significant depreciation discounts of 8–14%, and nearly identical tech on most mainstream trims. The smart buy for most buyers who prioritise total cost over novelty.

Head-to-Head: Six Categories That Matter

  • Safety Technology

    2025 Model
    • Standard camera-based driver monitoring across all trims
    • Upgraded AEB with intersection and turning detection
    • Rear cross-traffic braking now standard on base trim
    • Emergency Steering Assist added to mid and upper trims
    2024 Model
    • AEB standard — pedestrian and cyclist detection included
    • Lane keep assist and adaptive cruise on mid trims and above
    • Driver monitoring torque-only on most non-premium trims
    • Rear cross-traffic alert (no autonomous braking)
    🏆 2025 wins — meaningful safety suite upgrade across the board

  • Infotainment & Connectivity

    2025 Model
    • 12.3–15" touchscreen standard depending on trim
    • Wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto standard
    • Over-the-air software update capability
    • Faster GPU — sub-100ms screen response times
    • Native Google Maps integration on upper trims
    2024 Model
    • 10.25–12.3" screen standard; larger on premium trims
    • Wireless CarPlay on mid trim and above; wired on base
    • OTA updates available on top trim only
    • Slightly slower system response — noticeable on lower trims
    🏆 2025 wins — screen size, speed, and OTA parity improved significantly

  • Powertrain & Efficiency

    2025 Model
    • 2–4 mpg improvement on base hybrid variant
    • Revised transmission mapping — smoother low-speed response
    • 48V mild-hybrid option added to petrol base trim
    • Slightly quicker 0–60 on turbocharged variants (+0.2s)
    2024 Model
    • Proven reliability record — full-year owner data available
    • Virtually identical real-world performance on most trims
    • No mild-hybrid option on petrol base trim
    • Slightly higher fuel consumption on highway — 2–4 mpg
    🏆 2025 edges ahead — hybrid improvements justify upgrade for high-mileage drivers

  • Trim Levels & Standard Features

    2025 Model
    • Heated front seats now standard on base trim
    • Power-adjustable driver seat drops one trim level
    • Ambient interior lighting standard mid trim and above
    • Panoramic sunroof added as option on mid trim
    2024 Model
    • Heated seats require mid trim or higher
    • Similar feature set overall — differences are incremental
    • Available at top-trim spec now heavily discounted
    • Fewer ambient lighting options; no panoramic on mid
    🏆 2025 wins on paper — but 2024 top trim often matches 2025 mid trim at lower cost

  • Price & Depreciation

    2025 Model
    • MSRP 4–7% higher than equivalent 2024 trim
    • Will face its steepest depreciation in year one
    • Eligible for current incentives and financing offers
    • Higher resale floor in 3–5 years vs equivalent 2024
    2024 Model
    • 8–14% discount vs original MSRP now widely available
    • Year-one depreciation already absorbed by first owner
    • Dealer incentives aggressive — strong negotiating position
    • Lower absolute resale, but better cost-per-year ownership
    🏆 2024 wins clearly — year-one depreciation savings outweigh the feature gap for most buyers

  • Reliability & Ownership Data

    2025 Model
    • No full-year owner reliability data yet available
    • New infotainment hardware carries early-adopter risk
    • Warranty coverage begins fresh — full factory protection
    • 48V system is unproven at scale in this application
    2024 Model
    • 12+ months of owner data and independent reliability surveys
    • Known issues already addressed via TSB and software patch
    • Remaining factory warranty transferable on CPO examples
    • Proven powertrain — no new technology risk
    🏆 2024 wins — proven reliability record and full owner data are a genuine advantage

For most buyers, the 2024 model is the rational choice. For drivers who prioritise having the safest and most connected car on the road, the 2025 justifies its premium — but only just.

Which Year Is Right for You?

Buy the 2025 if…

  • Safety technology is your top priority — the ADAS improvements are substantive
  • You drive 20,000+ miles per year and will benefit from fuel efficiency gains
  • You plan to keep the car 6–8 years and want the latest software architecture
  • OTA updates and wireless connectivity are important to your daily workflow
  • You're buying base trim — the feature jump to 2025 base is most pronounced here

Buy the 2024 if…

  • Total cost of ownership over 3–5 years is your primary concern
  • You drive under 15,000 miles per year — fuel savings won't close the price gap
  • You want proven reliability data before committing to a new platform
  • You can access a Certified Pre-Owned 2024 with remaining factory warranty
  • Budget allows a higher 2024 trim for the same price as 2025 base or mid

Our Verdict

The 2025 sedan is the better car — that much is clear. Its safety system upgrades, across-the-board wireless connectivity, and efficiency improvements are genuine steps forward rather than cosmetic refreshes. But the 2024 is the better purchase for the majority of buyers. The $3,200–$5,800 price gap across trims is significant, the reliability track record is established, and CPO examples offer remaining warranty coverage at a meaningful discount. Unless safety technology or fuel efficiency gains are a priority for your usage, the 2024 delivers more value per dollar spent.
