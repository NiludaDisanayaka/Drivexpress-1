Overview

We put Lexus's five most family-oriented models through their paces — evaluating reliability, comfort, safety technology, and real-world practicality. Each vehicle was driven across city streets, motorways, and rural roads over three weeks. The 2025 lineup is arguably the brand's most refined to date, with renewed focus on hybrid powertrains and advanced driver-assistance systems.

Safety & Technology

Every model ships with Lexus Safety System+ 3.0 as standard — encompassing pre-collision warning with pedestrian and cyclist detection, radar cruise control, lane-keep assist, and the new Emergency Steering Assist. The updated 14-inch touchscreen, now powered by a dedicated graphics chip, is a genuine step forward. Wireless Apple CarPlay and Android Auto are standard across the entire range.

Our Verdict

Whether you're a growing family eyeing the spacious RX 500h, a city commuter drawn to the nimble UX, or a parent needing all seven seats in the TX — the 2025 Lexus lineup has a compelling answer. The brand's hybrid expertise shines throughout, delivering efficiency and performance without compromise.
