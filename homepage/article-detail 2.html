Overview

The electric vehicle market has shifted dramatically over the past 18 months. Range anxiety is fading, charging networks are maturing, and battery chemistry breakthroughs are delivering genuine value at competitive price points. This review covers the six most significant new EVs arriving in 2025 — assessed across range, charge speed, software integration, and ownership cost.

What's Changed in 2025

Ultra-Fast Charging

800V architectures are now mainstream, enabling 10–80% charges in under 18 minutes on compatible networks.

Solid-State Progress

Semi-solid-state cells debut in two models this year, promising 20% more energy density with improved thermal safety.

Smarter Battery Management

AI-driven BMS now predicts degradation cycles and optimises charge curves based on daily usage patterns.

Vehicle-to-Grid (V2G)

Three of the six models reviewed support bidirectional charging, turning the car into a home energy buffer.

What Buyers Should Know

  • 1

    Prioritise charging architecture over raw range. An 800V platform with 350 kW capability will serve you better day-to-day than an extra 30 miles of rated range on a 400V car.

  • 2

    Check real-world range at motorway speeds. Official WLTP figures can be 20–25% optimistic. Independent highway tests from 70 mph cruising are a far more honest benchmark.

  • 3

    Heat pump is non-negotiable in colder climates. Without one, range can drop by 35–40% in winter. All six models reviewed here include it as standard or a low-cost option.

  • 4

    Evaluate battery chemistry. LFP cells (Lithium Iron Phosphate) tolerate daily 100% charging with minimal degradation — ideal if your regular commute demands a full charge every night.

  • 5

    V2G can offset running costs significantly. If your energy tariff supports bidirectional export, models like the Kia EV9 and IONIQ 9 can earn back hundreds annually by feeding stored energy to the grid during peak hours.

Our Verdict

The IONIQ 9 takes top spot for balancing every metric a real-world buyer cares about, while the EQS remains the luxury benchmark. For families needing seven seats, the EV9 GT is unmatched. Whichever model fits your budget, the 2025 EV class represents the strongest case for making the switch yet — with charging infrastructure and software quality finally matching the hardware ambition.
